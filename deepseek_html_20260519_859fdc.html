Case 1: Acute Chest Pain

A 55-year-old man presents with sudden-onset central chest pain radiating to the left arm, associated with sweating and shortness of breath. ECG shows ST-segment elevation in leads II, III, aVF.
History: Patient is a chronic smoker, known hypertensive, and has a strong family history of ischemic heart disease. Pain started 2 hours ago at rest, described as "heavy pressure" 8/10.
Investigations: Troponin I elevated (12 ng/mL), CK-MB elevated. Chest X-ray shows mild pulmonary congestion. Echocardiogram reveals inferior wall hypokinesis.
Diagnosis: Acute inferior ST-elevation myocardial infarction (STEMI). Urgent reperfusion required.

Case 2: Progressive Dyspnea

A 68-year-old woman presents with worsening shortness of breath over 3 months, orthopnea, and bilateral leg swelling. She has a history of hypertension and type 2 diabetes. JVP is elevated, and crackles are heard at lung bases.
History: She reports reduced exercise tolerance, waking up at night gasping for air. No chest pain. She is on lisinopril and metformin. No prior cardiac workup.
Investigations: BNP markedly elevated (1200 pg/mL). Chest X-ray shows cardiomegaly and pulmonary vascular congestion. Echocardiogram: LVEF 35%, global hypokinesis, moderate mitral regurgitation.
Diagnosis: Congestive heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (HFrEF), likely ischemic cardiomyopathy given risk factors. Start diuretics, ACEi, beta-blockers.

Case 3: Fever and Headache

A 22-year-old college student presents with high fever (39.5°C), severe headache, photophobia, and neck stiffness for 2 days. No rash. He is drowsy but arousable.
History: No recent travel. Lives in a dormitory. A classmate had similar symptoms a week ago. No known sick contacts, no meningococcal vaccination.
Investigations: CSF analysis: cloudy, elevated WBCs (2000/µL, 90% neutrophils), low glucose (20 mg/dL), elevated protein. Gram stain shows Gram-negative diplococci.
Diagnosis: Bacterial meningitis, most likely Neisseria meningitidis. Start empiric ceftriaxone and dexamethasone. Droplet precautions.
